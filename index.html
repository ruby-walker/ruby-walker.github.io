    Building mutual respect...

    My practice is a judgment-free zone. I believe that no matter where you're from or what you've done, every human is valuable and capable of making positive changes in their life. As a queer woman of color that has dealt with her own mental health issues, I know what it's like to be seen as different. I will respect and validate your authentic self, no matter what you throw my way.

    Navigating life's challenges...

    I have experience helping people through life's toughest moments. The pain of grief, breakups, and abuse is always hard to deal with, but with the help of proven treatment strategies we can make our way to the other side whatever you might be facing.

    Cultivating growth...

    I believe that therapy is for everybody, whether you're dealing with a severe mental illness or just looking to improve yourself. I want to see you be the absolute best "you" you can be. Therapy can help you understand your emotions, raise your self-esteem, improve your relationships, and take your career to the next level. Together, we will take the steps you need to find real meaning in life and achieve your goals.

    Diverse Populations

    I have a background in social activism, and I am extremely knowledgeable on the real-world issues faced by today's diverse mental health consumers. I acknowledge the impact that racism and oppression has on my clients, and work with them to overcome those systemic barriers. Historically and today, minorities face barriers in accessing mental health treatment and finding practitioners that understand their struggles. I entered the field of mental health because I wanted to be there for people like me. And the truth is, having a clinician that you can empathize with and relate to makes a big difference in treatment. I specialize in work with the following populations...

    • Racial and ethnic minorities, including bi/multiracial people, dealing with external or internalized racism
    • Interracial couples, transracial adoption, and culturally-blended families
    • LGBTQ+ individuals and same-sex couples
    • Transgender, non-binary, and genderfluid individuals
    • Ethically non-monogamous (open or polyamorous) and kink-based relationships
    • College students and recent grads dealing with stressful life transitions
    • Children and adults with autism spectrum disorders or ADHD
    • People healing from sexual trauma, abusive relationships, or child abuse
    • People who have been abusive and want to make a change
    • People with a history of depression, anxiety, or suicidal thoughts
    • People with intrusive thoughts or violent sexual fantasies
    • People addicted to drugs, alcohol, sex, gambling, social media, or video games
    • People with severe mental illness including schizophrenia, bipolar disorder, OCD, and personality disorders
